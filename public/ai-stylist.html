AI Stylist

The stylist that knows your closet better than you do.

Macgie doesn't shuffle clothes at random. It reasons — over your taste, your body, the weather, the occasion, your mood and what's actually in your wardrobe — then hands you a look worth wearing, and tells you why it works.

Recommendation, not generation

This isn't an outfit randomizer.
It's a stylist that reasons.

Plenty of apps can throw a top and a bottom together. That's a shuffle, not styling. Macgie weighs everything a good stylist would notice about you and your day — then recommends the outfit that actually fits the moment.

Style preferences

The silhouettes, colors and vibes you gravitate toward — minimal, tailored, streetwear, soft. Your taste is the anchor for every pick.

Body profile

Proportion, fit and the shapes that flatter you. Macgie factors in what sits well on your frame, not just what matches on paper.

Weather

Today's real forecast — temperature, rain, wind. No linen in a cold snap, no wool in a heatwave. Comfort baked into every look.

Occasion

A pitch, a first date, a long-haul flight, a Sunday. Macgie reads the context and dresses you for where you're actually going.

Mood

Bold and seen, or quiet and comfortable? Tell Macgie how you want to feel and it styles to the energy, not just the calendar.

Wardrobe availability

Only clothes you actually own and can wear — nothing in the wash, nothing you've retired. Real outfits from your real closet.

Wear history

What you wore last week, what you love and what you keep skipping — so Macgie freshens the rotation instead of repeating it.

Context-aware

It dresses you for the day you're actually having.

A great outfit in theory can be the wrong outfit in practice. Macgie pulls in the signals a stylist would ask about before they'd ever open your closet.

  • Live weather — temperature, rain and wind shape the fabrics and layers.
  • Your calendar — a packed day of meetings gets dressed differently than a slow one.
  • The occasion — formality, dress codes and the room you're walking into.

Mood-aware styling

Some days you want to disappear. Some days you want the room.

The same wardrobe can say very different things. Macgie lets you steer the feeling — and reworks the recommendation around it in seconds.

  • "Make it a little bolder" — brighter color, sharper silhouette, more statement.
  • "Keep me comfortable" — soft layers, easy shapes, nothing you'll fight all day.
  • "Something that feels like me" — leans into the pieces you reach for on your best days.

The learning loop

It gets better every single day.

Macgie isn't fixed on day one. Every look you love, skip, tweak or actually wear teaches it something — quietly sharpening a private model of your taste.

  • Learns which combinations you accept and which you always swap out.
  • Notices seasonal shifts and how your taste evolves over time.
  • Rediscovers the forgotten 80% of your closet and works it back in.

The outfit explanation engine

Every look comes with the reason it works.

Macgie never asks you to just trust it. Each recommendation is explained in plain language — so you don't only get dressed, you learn to style yourself.

  • Color harmony — why these tones sit well together instead of clashing.
  • Silhouette balance — how the proportions play off each other on your frame.
  • Occasion fit — why this reads right for where you're headed.
  • Style principles — the small rules that make it look intentional, not accidental.

Inside the loop

How the learning loop works.

Three quiet steps, repeating in the background — turning a good first guess into styling that feels made for you.

1

Observe

Macgie watches the signals that matter — what you wore, what you loved, what you skipped, and how you tweaked each look before heading out.

2

Recommend

It weighs your taste, body, weather, occasion and mood against your available wardrobe, then proposes the outfit best suited to the moment — with the reasoning attached.

3

Learn

Your reaction feeds straight back in. The model sharpens overnight, so tomorrow's recommendations land closer than today's ever could.

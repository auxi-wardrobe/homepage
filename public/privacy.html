Privacy Policy

Last updated: July 2026

Your wardrobe is yours. This policy explains, in plain language, what information Macgie collects, how we use it, and the choices you have. We built Macgie to help you love what you already own — not to turn you into a product. We don't sell your personal information to advertisers.

1. Introduction 2. Information we collect 3. How we use it 4. Your wardrobe photos & data 5. AI processing 6. Sharing & disclosure 7. Data retention 8. Your rights & choices 9. Security 10. Children's privacy 11. International transfers 12. Changes to this policy 13. Contact us

1. Introduction

This Privacy Policy applies to the Macgie mobile app and the macgie.com website (together, the "Service"), operated by Macgie ("we", "us", "our"). By using the Service, you agree to the practices described here. If you don't agree, please don't use the Service. Where required by law, we rely on your consent or another lawful basis to process your information.

2. Information we collect

We collect only what we need to make Macgie work well for you:

  • Account information — such as your name, email address and sign-in credentials.
  • Wardrobe content — photos of your clothing, item details, tags, outfits, favorites and wear history.
  • Style preferences — the tastes, sizes and profile details you share so recommendations fit you.
  • Usage data — how you interact with the app, such as features used and looks you love or skip, so the Service improves.
  • Device & technical data — device model, app version, language and diagnostic logs.
  • Optional data — location or weather (only if you enable it) to tailor recommendations to your day.

3. How we use it

We use your information to:

  • Provide the core Service — building your digital wardrobe and recommending outfits.
  • Personalize styling to your taste, body, the weather and the occasion.
  • Visualize looks and process the photos you add.
  • Improve our features, models and reliability.
  • Provide support and respond to your requests.
  • Keep the Service secure and prevent abuse.
  • Send you service-related messages (and, only with your consent, product updates).

We do not use your wardrobe content to build advertising profiles, and we do not sell it.

4. Your wardrobe photos & data

The clothes you add, the outfits you build and the looks you save belong to you. We use this content solely to power your experience — organizing your wardrobe, generating recommendations and creating visualizations. We don't share your wardrobe photos publicly, and we don't hand them to advertisers. You can edit or delete individual items at any time, or remove your whole wardrobe by deleting your account.

5. AI processing

Macgie uses AI and machine-learning models to detect garments, remove backgrounds, tag items, generate recommendations and visualize outfits. Some of this processing happens on your device; some happens on our secure servers or with trusted processors acting on our instructions. We may use de-identified or aggregated data to improve model quality. Where we use third-party AI processors, they are bound by contract to protect your data and use it only to provide the service to us — not for their own purposes.

6. Sharing & disclosure

We share personal information only in limited circumstances:

  • Service providers — vetted vendors (hosting, analytics, AI processing) who act on our behalf under contract.
  • Legal reasons — when required by law, legal process, or to protect rights, safety and the integrity of the Service.
  • Business transfers — if Macgie is involved in a merger, acquisition or asset sale, with notice to you as required.
  • With your direction — when you choose to share a look or connect another service.

We do not sell your personal information, and we do not share it with advertisers for their own marketing.

7. Data retention

We keep your information for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide the Service. When you delete your account, we delete or de-identify your personal information within a reasonable period, except where we must retain it to meet legal, security or accounting obligations. Backups are purged on a rolling schedule.

8. Your rights & choices

Depending on where you live, you may have the right to access, correct, export or delete your personal information, and to object to or restrict certain processing. You can:

  • Access and edit your wardrobe and profile directly in the app.
  • Export your data or request a copy.
  • Delete your account, which removes your wardrobe content.
  • Manage notification and location permissions in your device settings.
  • Withdraw consent where processing is based on consent.

To exercise any right, contact us at privacy@macgie.com. We won't discriminate against you for using your rights.

9. Security

We protect your information with encryption in transit, access controls and other technical and organizational safeguards appropriate to the sensitivity of the data. No system is perfectly secure, but we work hard to keep your wardrobe safe and to respond quickly if something goes wrong.

10. Children's privacy

Macgie is not intended for children under 13 (or the minimum age required in your country). We don't knowingly collect personal information from children. If you believe a child has provided us information, contact us and we'll delete it.

11. International transfers

We may process and store your information in countries other than your own, including where our service providers operate. When we transfer personal information internationally, we use appropriate safeguards — such as standard contractual clauses — to protect it in line with applicable law.

12. Changes to this policy

We may update this policy from time to time. When we make material changes, we'll update the "Last updated" date and, where appropriate, notify you in the app. Your continued use of the Service after changes take effect means you accept the revised policy.

13. Contact us

Questions about your privacy or this policy? Reach our team at privacy@macgie.com. We read every message.
